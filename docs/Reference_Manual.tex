\section{Introduction}
Stop fighting with pure TikZ. mohelper is a LaTeX package designed to create professional Molecular Orbital (MO) diagrams with minimal code. It handles the positioning, electron population, and dashed connection lines automatically, so you can focus on the chemistry, not the coordinates.
Have a look:

\begin{figure}[h]
    \centering
    \begin{mohelper}
        \def\columndistance{3}
        \def\diagrammode{normal}
        \degenerate{0}{1s/up}{left}
        \degenerate{-2}{$\sigma$/pair}{middle}
        \degenerate{2}{$\sigma^*$/empty}{middle}
        \degenerate{0}{1s/up}{right}
        \connectorbital{0}{1}{-2}{1}{left}
        \connectorbital{0}{1}{2}{1}{left}
        \connectorbital{-2}{1}{0}{1}{right}
        \connectorbital{2}{1}{0}{1}{right}
        \addenergyscale{0}
    \end{mohelper}
    \caption{Hydrogen Molecule}
    \label{fig:modiagram1}
\end{figure}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\begin{mohelper}
	\def\columndistance{3}
	\def\diagrammode{normal}
	\degenerate{0}{1s/up}{left}
	\degenerate{-2}{$\sigma$/pair}{middle}
	\degenerate{2}{$\sigma^*$/empty}{middle}
	\degenerate{0}{1s/up}{right}
	\connectorbital{0}{1}{-2}{1}{left}
	\connectorbital{0}{1}{2}{1}{left}
	\connectorbital{-2}{1}{0}{1}{right}
	\connectorbital{2}{1}{0}{1}{right}
	\addenergyscale{0}
\end{mohelper}
\end{lstlisting}

\section{Quick Start}
To use the diagrams generated by this tool in your LaTeX project, follow these steps:
\subsection{Include the Package}
Download the package and include it into your project. To do so download the \texttt{mohelper.sty} file from
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \href{https://github.com/thevicraft/modiagram-latex-integration/releases}{Releases} (recommended, stable)
    \item \href{https://thevicraft.github.io/modiagram-latex-integration/}{Repository} (only if you know what you are doing)
\end{enumerate}
and paste the file into your project's root directory. Ensure you have the mohelper package included in your preamble:
\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\input{mohelper.sty}
\end{lstlisting}
If you put \texttt{mohelper.sty} into a subdirectory, you must specify its path. Therefore, have this example shown:
\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\input{./lib/mohelper.sty}
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Configure your Diagram}
Open the \href{https://thevicraft.github.io/modiagram-latex-integration/}{Web Helper} and set up your orbitals, energies, and labels.

\subsection{Copy and Paste}
Copy the generated LaTeX code and paste it into your \texttt{figure} environment or document body.

\section{Basic Usage}
The heart of the package is the \texttt{mohelper} environment. It follows a three-column logic: \textbf{Left (AO) | Middle (MO) | Right (AO)}.

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \begin{mohelper}
        \def\columndistance{3}
        \def\diagrammode{normal}
        \degenerate{0}{1s/up}{left}
        \degenerate{-2}{$\sigma$/pair}{middle}
        \degenerate{2}{$\sigma^*$/empty}{middle}
        \degenerate{0}{1s/up}{right}
        \connectorbital{0}{1}{-2}{1}{left}
        \connectorbital{0}{1}{2}{1}{left}
        \connectorbital{-2}{1}{0}{1}{right}
        \connectorbital{2}{1}{0}{1}{right}
        \addlabel{\color{blue}{left},\color{blue}{middle},\color{blue}{right}}{0}
        \addenergyscale{0}
    \end{mohelper}
    \label{fig:modiagram2}
\end{figure}

\section{Macro Reference}

\subsection{The Environment}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\begin{mohelper}[options]
    ...
\end{mohelper}
\end{lstlisting}

Wraps a \texttt{tikzpicture}. You can pass standard TikZ options like \texttt{[scale=0.8]}.

\subsection{Defining Orbitals}

\paragraph{\textbackslash degenerate}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\degenerate{energy}{label1/spin1, label2/spin2, ...}{side}
\end{lstlisting}

\noindent The \texttt{\textbackslash degenerate} command is your Swiss Army knife. It automatically calculates the centering for multiple orbitals.

\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|c| p{7cm} |p{3cm}|}
        \hline
        Parameter    & Description                                                                              & Example                                                                                                                                                                              \\ [0.5ex]
        \hline%\hline
        \texttt{\#1} & \textbf{Y-Level} (Energy)                                                                & \texttt{-2.5}                                                                                                                                                                        \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#2} & \textbf{Label/Spin Array} (Format: \texttt{label1\slash spin1, label2\slash spin2, ...}) & \texttt{\$p\_x\$\slash pair, \$p\_y\$\slash up, \$p\_z\$\slash up} \colorbox{lightgray}{\begin{mohelper}[scale=0.8]\degenerate{0}{$p_x$/pair,$p_y$/up,$p_z$/up}{left}\end{mohelper}}
        \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#3} & Placement                                                                                & \texttt{left}, \texttt{middle} or \texttt{right}                                                                                                                                     \\ [1ex]
        \hline
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Supported Spins}: \texttt{up}, \texttt{down}, \texttt{pair} (any other value will result in an empty orbital)

\paragraph{\textbackslash orbital}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\orbital{x}{y}{label}{spin}
\end{lstlisting}

\noindent \texttt{\textbackslash orbital} manually draws a single orbital at the specified coordinates. For standard orbital diagrams it should not be necessary to use this macro. Only use \texttt{\textbackslash degenerate}.

\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|c| p{7cm} |p{3cm}|}
        \hline
        Parameter    & Description                    & Example                                                                                       \\ [0.5ex]
        \hline%\hline
        \texttt{\#1} & \textbf{x} coordinate          & \texttt{4.2}                                                                                  \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#2} & \textbf{y} coordinate (energy) & {2.0}
        \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#3} & Label                          & \texttt{1s}                                                                                   \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#4} & Spin                           & \texttt{up}, \texttt{down} or \texttt{pair} (any other value will result in an empty orbital) \\ [1ex]
        \hline
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{Connecting Orbitals}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\connectorbital{left-E}{left-deg}{right-E}{right-deg}{side}
\end{lstlisting}

\newcommand{\previewdrawsmall}[1]{
    \colorbox{lightgray}{
        \centering
        \begin{mohelper}[scale=0.4]
            \def\columndistance{0.3}
            \degenerate{0}{/o}{left}
            \degenerate{-2.3}{/o}{middle}
            \degenerate{0}{/o}{right}
            \ifstrequal{#1}{left}{
                \connectorbital{0}{1}{-2.3}{1}{left}
            }{
                \connectorbital{-2.3}{1}{0}{1}{right}
            }
        \end{mohelper}
    }
}

\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|c| p{7cm} |c|}
        \hline
        Parameter    & Description                                     & Example                                            \\ [0.5ex]
        \hline%\hline
        \texttt{\#1} & \textbf{Energy} of \textbf{left} orbital        & \texttt{-5.3}                                      \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#2} & \textbf{Degeneration} of \textbf{left} orbital  & \texttt{1}                                         \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#3} & \textbf{Energy} of \textbf{right} orbital       & \texttt{2.5}                                       \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#4} & \textbf{Degeneration} of \textbf{right} orbital & \texttt{3}                                         \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#5} & \textbf{Placement} of the connection line       & \begin{tabular}{c c}

                                                                             \texttt{left}           & \texttt{right}           \\
                                                                             \hline
                                                                             \previewdrawsmall{left} & \previewdrawsmall{right} \\
                                                                         \end{tabular}
        \\[1ex]
        \hline
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}

By specifying a side (\texttt{left} or \texttt{right}) in \texttt{\#5} the connection line is drawn between either
\begin{itemize}
    \item \texttt{left}: left AO and MO
    \item \texttt{right}: MO and right AO
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Customization}
You can tweak the look for each diagram by redefining these values:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \texttt{\textbackslash def\textbackslash columndistance\{value\}}: Set \texttt{value} to a number. It defines space between the AO und MO columns.
    \item \texttt{\textbackslash def\textbackslash diagrammode\{compact\}}: Switches to vertical stacking for degenerate orbitals (perfect for large systems).
          \newcommand{\previewcompact}[1]{
              \begin{mohelper}[scale=0.6]
                  \def\diagrammode{#1}
                  \ifstrequal{#1}{compact}{\def\electroncolor{red}}{}
                  \overlapp{-0.5/{2s/pair},2/{2p/pair,2p/up,2p/up}}{-3/{$\sigma$/pair},6/{$\sigma^*$/leer},1/{/pair}}{3/{/pair,/leer}}%a1
                  \overlapp{2/{,,}}{4/{$\pi^*$/leer},-1/{$\pi$/pair}}{3/{,}}%b1
                  \overlapp{2/{,,}}{2/{/pair}}{}%b2
                  \addlabel{$\ce{O}$,$\ce{H2O}$,$\ce{H2}$}{0.5}
              \end{mohelper}
          }
          \begin{center}
              \begin{tabular}{|c c|}
                  \hline
                  \texttt{normal}                & \texttt{compact}         \\
                  \hline
                  \previewcompact{normal} \vline & \previewcompact{compact} \\
                  \hline
              \end{tabular}
          \end{center}
    \item \texttt{\textbackslash def\textbackslash electroncolor\{color\}}: Set \texttt{color} to a valid color value. Changes the electron color to a specified one. The redefining has to stand before the relevant orbital is created using \texttt{\textbackslash degenerate}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Miscellaneous}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\degeneratelabel{energy}{deg}{side}{align}{text}
\end{lstlisting}
Draws a label for a group of degenerated orbitals. This is an alternate macro if the label is not specified in \texttt{\textbackslash degenerate}. Furthermore, the label is intended to be describing all degenerated orbitals of concern. Here, you can specify the text position, very helpful if you want the label next to the degenerated orbitals (see param \texttt{\#4}).

\newcommand{\previewdegeneratelabel}[1]{
    \colorbox{lightgray}{
        \begin{mohelper}[scale=0.6]
            \degenerate{0}{/pair}{left}
            \degeneratelabel{0}{1}{left}{left}{}
            \degeneratelabel{0}{1}{left}{right}{}
            \degeneratelabel{0}{1}{left}{bottom}{}
            \degeneratelabel{0}{1}{left}{top}{}
            \degeneratelabel{0}{1}{left}{#1}{$\color{blue}{1a_{1g}}$}
        \end{mohelper}
    }
}

\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|c| p{7cm} |c|}
        \hline
        Parameter    & Description                                & Example                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                \\ [0.5ex]
        \hline%\hline
        \texttt{\#1} & \textbf{Energy} of orbital                 & \texttt{-13.6}                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#2} & \textbf{Degeneration} of orbital           & \texttt{1}                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#3} & \textbf{Column} of orbital                 & \begin{tabular}{c c}

                                                                        \texttt{left} & left AO \\  \hline\texttt{middle} & MO\\ \hline                  \texttt{right} & right AO\\
                                                                    \end{tabular}                                                                                                                                              \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#4} & \textbf{Text Position} relative to orbital & \begin{tabular}{c c}

                                                                        \texttt{left} & \previewdegeneratelabel{left} \\  \hline\texttt{right} & \previewdegeneratelabel{right}\\ \hline                  \texttt{bottom} & \previewdegeneratelabel{bottom}\\  \hline             \texttt{top} & \previewdegeneratelabel{top}  \\
                                                                    \end{tabular} \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#5} & \textbf{Text Content}                      &
        \\[1ex]
        \hline
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\addenergyscale{offset}
\end{lstlisting}
Adds an energy scale. \texttt{offset} (number) specifies the additional horizontal offset from the diagram. As a default, just set it to \texttt{0}.
{\color{red}{This macro has to be executed after each orbital is drawn. Otherwise the scale's size and position will be calculated incorrect.}}

\paragraph{}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\addlabel{label1, label2, label3}{offset}
\end{lstlisting}
Gives all three columns a label (left AOs, MOs, right AOs). \texttt{offset} (number) specifies the additional vertical offset from the diagram. As a default, just set it to \texttt{0}.
{\color{red}{This macro has to be executed after each orbital is drawn. Otherwise the vertical position will be calculated incorrect.}}

\begin{lstlisting}[language=TeX]
\showenergydifference{energy1, energy2}{side}{offset}{text}
\end{lstlisting}

\noindent Draws a double-sided red arrow pointing out an energy difference. \texttt{offset} (number) specifies an additional horizontal offset. As a default, just set it to \texttt{0} which will center it.

\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|c| p{7cm} |p{3cm}|}
        \hline
        Parameter    & Description                                               & Example                                           \\ [0.5ex]
        \hline%\hline
        \texttt{\#1} & \textbf{Energy Array} (Format: \texttt{energy1, energy2}) & \texttt{2.0, 4.0}                                 \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#2} & \textbf{Column} of orbital                                & \texttt{2.0}
        \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#3} & \textbf{Horizontal offset}, moves the arrow along x-axis  & \texttt{0} (centered)                             \\
        \hline
        \texttt{\#4} & \textbf{Label}                                            & \texttt{\$\textbackslash Delta\_O\$ ($\Delta_O$)} \\ [1ex]
        \hline
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}
